Discover Conway, SC

South Carolina's
Hidden Gem

One of the oldest towns in South Carolina, Conway blends historic rivertown charm with modern convenience — just 15 miles from the Grand Strand.

Outdoor & Nature

The Waccamaw Riverwalk

Conway's crown jewel is its scenic Riverwalk along the black waters of the Waccamaw River, featuring historic markers, public artwork, a marina, and a public park. It is the heartbeat of the community and the perfect place for a morning stroll or sunset gathering.

For adventure seekers, Black River Outdoors offers guided kayaking tours on the Waccamaw, where paddlers explore the area's stunning natural beauty while learning about the river's rich ecosystem from experienced local guides.

History & Culture

250+ Years of Heritage

Conway's walkable downtown is a treasure trove of Southern history and creativity. The Horry County Museum preserves the region's rich heritage from Native American artifacts through the Textile Era, while the L.W. Paul Living History Farm lets visitors experience daily life on an early South Carolina homestead.

The arts scene thrives with Conway Glass — an artisan studio where visitors watch skilled glassblowers at work — and the Theatre of the Republic, hosting Broadway-style musicals and community productions in the heart of downtown.

Food & Retail

Dining & Shopping

Conway's dining scene punches well above its weight, from the upscale Lowcountry cuisine at Rivertown Bistro to the laid-back river views at Bonfire Taqueria, founded by well-known local restaurateur Darren Smith. Whitaker's and Hop N' Wich round out the culinary landscape with creative, locally-driven menus.

Shopping downtown means browsing the Black Water Market, exploring unique boutiques and galleries, and discovering one-of-a-kind finds in a charming, walkable setting that feels worlds away from the chain stores of the beach.

Education & Community

A Place to Call Home

Conway is widely regarded as a safe, welcoming community ideal for raising a family. Strong local schools provide quality education from elementary through high school, and the area is home to Coastal Carolina University — bringing collegiate energy, events, and Division I athletics to the community.

Horry Georgetown Technical College offers additional higher-education opportunities, while year-round community events, festivals, and a tight-knit neighborhood atmosphere make Conway the kind of place where people put down roots and stay.
